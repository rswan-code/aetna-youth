Pressure Test
The hypothesis held. The data delivers.
Aetna markets purchased the BH archetype for youth Medicaid populations based on a clinical hypothesis: that youth in Medicaid carry significant, unmeasured loneliness and behavioral health burden that standard engagement programs miss.
Confirmed: Across all 5 markets, youth UCLA-3 scores range from 4.3 to 5.2 — every market above the clinical threshold for meaningful loneliness. 32% of youth meet a loneliness threshold. 72% of those youth also screen positive for psychological distress. 30% carry an identified social need. This is not a margin-of-error finding. This is a population-level behavioral health burden that was invisible before this program.

Pressure Test
Guardians were the missing link.
The BH Guardian program identified a population that exists in every Medicaid plan but appears in almost no engagement model: the adults raising children placed by the state.
The compound picture: Guardian avg age is 40–42. Pennsylvania Family Insight
PA is the equity and depth market.
Two signals define the PA family program. First, health equity: 24% of PA youth identify discrimination as a health barrier — the highest in the portfolio. This is a quality and equity metric that HEDIS cannot see but drives real care avoidance. Second, program depth: PA guardian launched Dec 2023, conducts PRAPARE + Well-Being assessments, and connects 85% of guardians to Aetna Better Health Kids plan resources. PA shows what a mature, comprehensive family program looks like — and the equity signals it surfaces.
