Across five markets, Pyx Health's Family Connection and Well-Being Program has enrolled 6,867 members — 5,622 youth ages 13–17 and 1,245 caregivers and guardians — because barriers to care don't start with the child. They start in the household.

Youth and guardian members qualify through Aetna's target list, ensuring the right families are reached from day one. The data below captures leading indicators for both populations — what Pyx is generating now is the clinical visibility Aetna needs to act before these members become high-cost adults. See the Guardian Network tab for full caregiver data by market.

What's Keeping Youth from Care
Youth barriers differ substantially from adult barriers — and from market to market. Social media emerges as a top barrier in Texas and Pennsylvania, a leading indicator of digital isolation. Family dynamics dominate in Florida and Oklahoma. Safety concerns surface in every market. These findings require youth-specific navigation, not adult protocols.

  • Social media as the #1 barrier in Texas and Pennsylvania is a finding unique to youth programs — it signals digital isolation, not engagement, and requires a fundamentally different navigation approach than adult SDOH protocols
