Stage 3 — Barrier Identification
What's Keeping Youth from Care
Youth barriers differ substantially from adult barriers — and from market to market. Social media emerges as a top barrier in Texas and Pennsylvania, a leading indicator of digital isolation. Family dynamics dominate in Florida and Oklahoma. Safety concerns surface in every market. These findings require youth-specific navigation, not adult protocols.

Talking Points — Youth Barriers

  • Social media as the #1 barrier in Texas and Pennsylvania is a finding unique to youth programs — it signals digital isolation, not engagement, and requires a fundamentally different navigation approach than adult SDOH protocols
  • Safety concerns appear in the top four barriers across all five markets — these youth are navigating instability at home and in their communities simultaneously
  • Discrimination at 18% in Florida is the highest rate in the portfolio — a health equity signal that belongs in Aetna FL's equity reporting and SDOH documentation
  • These barrier maps give Aetna's SDOH and quality teams actionable intelligence they cannot generate from claims data alone
